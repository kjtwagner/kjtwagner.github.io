Lecture in AP Computer Science Principles relating course content to topics in astronomy, particularly big data and LIGO.


---
One unit in AP Computer Science Principles discusses big data in the modern world. Astronomy is one field where big data presents some problems. While the amount of data becoming available throuhg surveys like SDSS, Pan-STARRS, and from new observatories like the Vera C. Rubin, it also introduces the question of how to parse through massive amounts of information to find meaning in a sea of data.

In this talk, I introduced the problem, discussed some astronomical data sets, and gave an overview of a dew of the ways astronomers deal with big data. I then moved into a discussion relating my research with LIGO to the course material - explaining how interferometers work, how LIGO collects, stores, and analyzes data, and how I have worked on a improving a specific search method that scales computational cost with search sensitivity.
